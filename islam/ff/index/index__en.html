Introduction to course
The term ecology (from the Greek oikos - house, dwelling, residence and logos - doctrine) as it was introduced in 1886 by the German biologist Ernest Haeckel, means the study of the general relationships of animals and plants as with living (biotic), and abiotic (abiotic) nature, including all the friendly and unfriendly relations with which animals and plants directly or indirectly come into contact. In other words, ecology in the classical sense is the science of the interaction of living organisms among themselves and their environment.
